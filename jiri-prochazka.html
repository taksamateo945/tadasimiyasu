Jiri Prochazka - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, Single, Nationality, Body Measurement, Career

Jiri Prochazka is a rising professional mixed martial artist from the Czech Republic. The multi-talented fighter has also competed in Muay Thai kickboxing as an amateur. Since 2020, Jiri Prochazka has competed in the Ultimate Fighting Championships. Jiri also won gold at the Czech National Championships. He was also a floorball enthusiast and a freestyle BMX rider.

Early Life

Jiri Prochazka was born in Znojmo, Czech Republic, on October 14, 1992. Jiri came to prominence only after a promising career as an MMA fighter, so little is known about his parents and early life. When he was six years old, his father died. Before switching to Muay Thai, he played football for a local team in his teens. He is 29 years old. 

Professional Life

Jiri began his training at a young age. He began his career in Muay Thai, then moved on to Kickboxing. Procházka won the national Muay Thai championship in 2011. Jiri turned professional in 2012 and made his debut against Stanislav Futera, winning by KO in the first round. In December 2012, the fighter lost his first professional match in SFC: Balkan Fighter Night to Bojan Velickovic.

Jiri defeated Martin Solc in the inaugural GCF Light Heavyweight Championship in December 2013. In the following matches, he successfully defended his title. The fighter debuted at heavyweight in the Rizin World Grand Prix in 2015. He was defeated in the series final by Muhammed Lawal. Prochazka defeated Muhammed Lawal in the inaugural Rizin FF Light Heavyweight Championship in 2019.

Jiri defended his title in the Rizin 20 in 2019. He signed with the UFC in early 2020 and made his debut against Volkan Oezdemir in UFC 251. Prochazka won the match and Performance of the Night by KO in the second round. Jiri was supposed to fight Dominick Reyes at UFC Fight Night 186, but Reyes pulled out due to injury, and the fight has been rescheduled for May 1.
